# The General Business-Owner Archetype

*Archetype · Universe: agency client roster*

An archetype representing the broader audience [[nathan]]'s landing page converts — any business owner who needs video content and isn't sure who to trust with it.

---

## Relation to the central idea

Umbrella audience for [[02-talk-with-flavour-landing-page]]. Restaurants are the sharpest specific case ([[restaurant-owner-archetype]]); cafés, bars, food brands, retail, service businesses, artisans and lifestyle operators share the same decision pattern. Page design decisions that serve this archetype serve the restaurant case too.

## Wound

Craft-quality video feels out of reach. Either they tried before and the result looked amateur, or the vendor didn't understand the business. Now they hesitate — the cost of a second bad experience is reputational, not just financial.

## Want

Video that makes the business look as good as it actually is. Content that works across social, paid, and owned channels without looking stitched together.

## Need

**Reputation signals they can trust inside 90 seconds.** Evidence that this videographer *gets* businesses like theirs. Past work in their vertical. Proof of operational sensitivity. Pricing they don't have to chase.

## Weakness

- 90-second decision window
- Decides on gut, validated by signals
- Under-weights aesthetic quality; over-weights "will this person be a headache on site"
- Risk-averse when the brand is theirs to protect

## Motivations

- Stronger positioning in a crowded vertical
- Higher-ticket customers, larger contracts, better booking mix
- Content that compounds across channels
- Protecting the brand they've built

## Support system

- Business partner, ops lead, social/marketing consultant — all with opinions

## Appearance in the world

Time-poor. Operationally conservative. Privileged by owning something worth filming; under-privileged in production literacy. Responds to evidence faster than to claims.

---

## Decision factors (ranked)

1. Past work matching their vertical's positioning
2. Evidence of operational sensitivity — won't disrupt the business
3. Clear, unhidden pricing
4. Low booking friction
5. Brand warmth — distant last
