# The Resource Curator

*Archetype · Universe: [[command-centre]] · Hero of [[02b-products-that-sell]]*

The intended user of [[study-buddy]]. Every product decision in Chapter 2b tests against whether it serves this character. Not a student in general; a specific kind of expert who wants to share what they know.

---

## Relation to the central idea

The Resource Curator is the commercial expression of *build should feel like play* pointed at a different room. They have already done the play — the vault. What they cannot do alone is turn their play into a shareable tool for others. Study-buddy exists so their play produces a sellable artifact. That is the campaign thesis in a second voice.

## Wound

Hundreds of hours inside their own Obsidian vault, a knowledge base that is real, structured, and useful — but locked behind a tool and a plugin stack most of their audience will not install. Their expertise compounds in private. When they want to share it, the act of sharing requires the recipient to become an Obsidian user. That friction is the wound: the better the vault gets, the less portable it is.

## Want

A tool that lets them *send* their vault to students or peers without those students having to install anything. A URL. A player. Something as easy to open as a PDF reader, as structured as a flashcard deck, as private as a local file.

## Need

Distribution that respects both their intellectual property and the privacy of their audience. *Privacy-first distribution* is not a feature to them; it is the moral shape of their work. They do not want a SaaS to own their students' study data. They do not want their vault sitting on a platform they do not control. The need is not a tool; it is a *posture*.

## Weakness

Over-curation. They keep refining their vault instead of shipping it. A platform that lowers the ship-cost risks also lowering the *refine-cost*, turning a valuable discipline into perpetual polish. The product has to resist giving them one more reason to delay release.

## Motivations

- Turn private expertise into a distributable good without becoming a SaaS operator
- Preserve the data-sovereignty ethos that made them trust Obsidian in the first place
- Professionalise their knowledge base without over-investing in the platformisation of their work
- Reach an audience — often a niche, deeply-engaged one — with something they can actually use

## Character-defining moments

- Sending a raw markdown folder and receiving *how do I open this?* as a reply
- Watching a recipient install Obsidian, install the spaced-repetition plugin, fail to parse the vault's custom frontmatter, and give up
- Realising their vault is more valuable than their notes — because it is *curated*, and the curation is what their audience actually wants
- *(Extended as real curators onboard)*

## Support system

- A small audience or mailing list, often already built on a single niche topic
- A handful of peers in the same field, usually remote
- No institutional distribution — by definition, this is work that sits outside traditional publishing, course platforms, or academic channels

## Appearance in the world

Usually older than the Obsidian power-user stereotype by five to fifteen years. Often a practitioner first, writer second — a blockchain engineer who teaches blockchain, a comedy writer who teaches comedy, a legal specialist who teaches a sub-discipline. Operates from their own workstation, reads carefully, writes in plain text. Owns their own domain. Uses browsers as tools, not consumption surfaces.

## Archetype constraints (read before major product decisions)

1. If the answer requires the recipient to install anything, the answer is wrong.
2. If the answer stores the curator's vault on the operator's servers, the answer is wrong.
3. If the answer treats study progress as platform data rather than user data, the answer is wrong.
4. If the answer optimises for SaaS pricing (per-seat, per-vault) rather than for the curator's distribution posture, the answer is wrong.
5. If the answer makes the curator feel like their work is being platformised away from them, the answer is wrong.

The archetype's job is to say no to answers that would turn study-buddy into another platform the curator does not trust.

## Notes

The Resource Curator is a *smaller* market than "students" or "note-takers" and a *sharper* one. The catalogue's Option II (free core + custom-branded players) is the monetisation path that matches this archetype; Option I (free OSS only) matches the community-validation phase before monetisation.
