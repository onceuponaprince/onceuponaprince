Why This Palette?

Green represents flavour, freshness, and identity — but here it's treated as an atmosphere, not a brand paint. The deep forest tones are immersive without being oppressive; they reference the specificity of serious kitchens, not generic "food" branding.

The lime accent is deployed like a seasoning — present enough to define, absent enough to matter. Cream type over dark green creates immediate premium legibility without the clinical cold of pure black-on-white.

Instrument Serif

A refined, optical-size aware serif from Google. Used exclusively in italic at display sizes. It carries editorial weight and warmth — evoking quality print, not corporate polish. Appears in headlines, pull quotes, and brand marks only.

Cabinet Grotesk

A contemporary geometric grotesque from Fontshare. Distinctive without being eccentric — highly legible at small sizes, confident at large ones. The Black weight at all-caps creates structural tension against the fluid italic serif.

The Rule of Contrast

These two fonts should never compete. The serif owns italics, display sizes, and emotional moments. The grotesque owns uppercase labels, body copy, and navigation. Their contrast creates the editorial premium feel without overdesigning.

Favicon / Monogram Mark

The monogram uses a bold T-form (T for Talk) with a lime accent dot — referencing both the brand initial and the concept of flavour as a final, deliberate touch.

✓  Do

  • Use the wordmark in Cabinet Grotesk Black (900 weight)
  • Maintain the tag line in Instrument Serif Italic beneath when space allows
  • Apply on the four approved background variants only
  • Use minimum 16px clear space around the mark on all sides
  • Scale the lime accent dot proportionally with the monogram

×  Don't

  • Recolour the wordmark — only cream, deep green, or lime are permitted
  • Add drop shadows, gradients, or glow effects to the logo
  • Stretch, skew, or alter the letter spacing
  • Place the mark on busy photographic backgrounds without an overlay
  • Use the monogram alone when the full wordmark fits the space

04 / Layout & Spacing Principles

01

Content Before Chrome

Navigation, labels, and UI chrome should recede. The content — video, interview, story — is the primary visual. Navigation is minimal, always fixed, always small. No decorative elements compete with the work.

02

One Focal Point Per Screen

Every viewport has one dominant element: a headline, a video, a founder portrait. The eye is guided without being pushed. Secondary elements exist only to support the primary — never to compete with it.

03

The Editorial Rule

Sections alternate rhythm: full-bleed moment → narrow text → asymmetric two-column → full-bleed. This creates a reading journey, not a template. No two adjacent sections share the same column structure.

04

Generous Vertical Space

Section padding: clamp(64px, 8vw, 128px). White space is confidence. Empty areas are not to be filled — they let premium content breathe and create the feeling of intention over volume.

05

Neobrutalist Detail

The brutalist signature: hard edges (border-radius: 0–4px), visible structural borders in low-opacity cream, and offset box-shadows using the lime accent. Applied with restraint — never on more than 2–3 elements per page.

06

4px Spacing System

All spacing derived from a 4px base grid. No arbitrary values. The eye registers harmonic proportion even when it can't name it — and breaks in the grid register as carelessness.

06 / Voice, Tone & Copy Rules

The Enquiry Prompt

"Tell me about your concept."

Not "Fill in the form below." The voice is curious and direct — treating founders like the intelligent operators they are.

The Hero Line

"Where hospitality founders find their voice."

Simple. Specific. No superlatives, no "empowering" language. It describes what happens here — not what we think of ourselves.

Section Labels

"Founders. Stories. Platform."

Single nouns, no filler. Navigation and section headings are declarative — never questions, never sales copy.

CTA Copy

"Start a conversation."

Avoids "Sign up", "Subscribe", "Get started". Every action feels like an invitation, not a form submission.

Episode / Content Title

"Tunde Wey on feeding the neighbourhood."

Name first — the founder is the subject. The concept follows. No clickbait, no "You won't believe…" The work earns the click.

About / Mission Line

"Built for the people who run the room."

Insider language that hospitality people recognise. Signals specialist knowledge, not consumer positioning.

✓  Write

  • Short. Declarative. Present tense.
  • Industry-literate — use real hospitality terms (concept, covers, front-of-house)
  • Name the founder early — people, not products
  • One clear idea per sentence
  • Active voice throughout

×  Avoid

  • "Empowering", "elevating", "unlocking potential"
  • Exclamation marks
  • Passive constructions ("Stories are told here")
  • Jargon from outside the industry (KPIs, synergy, ecosystem)
  • Generic food metaphors ("feast your eyes", "a taste of…")

07 / Digital Applications

Motion Spec

Standard Easing

cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)

The golden curve. Used for all interactive hover/focus transitions. Fast out, slow settle. Feels intentional, never bouncy.

Duration — Micro

180ms

Button hover states, input focus rings, badge colour shifts. Below human perception threshold for latency.

Duration — Element

320–400ms

Modal opens, card expand, section reveal. Perceptible but never sluggish.

Scroll Reveal

opacity 0 → 1

CSS scroll-driven animations only. Opacity reveals — no translate (prevents CLS). Content enters calmly, never dramatically.

Neobrutalist Hover

box-shadow offset ↑

Primary buttons: shadow grows from 4px to 6px offset on hover, background inverts to transparent. Tactile without animation overkill.

Reduced Motion

prefers-reduced-motion

All transitions collapse to 0.01ms. Static state is always the accessible fallback — the design must look complete without motion.

Grid Pattern: 1–2–3 rhythm

  • 01 Every 3rd post is a title card — wordmark or brand message on deep green. This anchors the grid identity.
  • 02 Portrait / video posts: dark green or near-black background where possible, subject centred and well-lit.
  • 03 Text graphics: Cream type on forest green, a single editorial serif quote. No more than 2 lines of copy.
