As a research subagent for Episode 2 of the agent architecture routing thread, I have compiled the following analysis from academic literature and systems research spanning 2024 through early 2026.

### 1. PREDICTIVE FEATURES
The literature identifies a shift from "black-box" model selection to **structural task-feature engineering**.

*   **Domain-Action Taxonomy:** Research on **Arch-Router** (Katanemo, 2025) demonstrates that the most predictive features are not the raw prompt, but the mapping of queries to specific *Domains* (e.g., Finance, Code) and *Actions* (e.g., Extraction, Synthesis).
*   **Internal Difficulty Representations:** Foster et al. (2026, arXiv) identified that LLMs encode "linearly accessible internal representations of difficulty." Routers can now probe the *pre-generation activations* of a small model to decide if a task is "too hard," essentially using the model's own uncertainty as a routing signal.
*   **Task Horizon and State Coupling:** **MasRouter** (Yan et al., ACL 2025) emphasizes that **collaboration mode** (Chain vs. Mesh topology) is more predictive of success than simple model capability. High state-coupling tasks require "Mesh" routing, while independent sub-tasks are parallelizable.
*   **Runtime Probing vs. Inference:** While basic intent is inferred from the task statement, **RouteLLM** (LMSYS, 2024) utilizes "Matrix Factorization" (MF) to score prompt-model fit, which requires historical trace data rather than just runtime text analysis.

### 2. TRAINING DATA
The scarcity of routing data has been mitigated by the release of massive "trace-centric" datasets.

*   **HAL (Holistic Agent Leaderboard):** The Princeton PLI (2025) released the HAL dataset, providing **2.5 Billion tokens of agent logs** and 21,730 full trajectories across benchmarks like **tau-bench, AgentBench, and GAIA**. This is the "ImageNet moment" for routing data.
*   **Trajectory Synthesis:** **ToolACE-MCP** (2025) demonstrated that "random walk" sampling of tool subsets can synthesize context-rich trajectories for training routers when production logs are unavailable.
*   **TRouter Data Synthesis:** **TRouter** (April 2026) uses a "task-profile-guided data synthesis" framework to solve the **cold-start problem**, allowing a solo founder to generate training data for a new model without thousands of real-world interactions.

### 3. CLASSIFIER SHAPE
The "cheapest" effective classifier has converged on two specific architectures:

*   **The 1.5B Parameter "Small" Model:** **Arch-Router** utilizes a 1.5B parameter generative model that maps queries to policies in ~51ms. This is the current sweet spot for latency vs. accuracy.
*   **Cascaded Controller Networks:** **MasRouter** (ACL 2025) employs a three-layer cascade: 
    1.  *Collaboration Mode Determiner* (Variational Latent Variable Model).
    2.  *Role Allocator* (Probabilistic Cascade).
    3.  *LLM Router* (Multinomial Distribution selection).
*   **Matrix Factorization (MF):** RouteLLM proves that a simple MF scoring function often outperforms complex BERT classifiers in cost-performance ratios for model-selection tasks.

### 4. MEASUREMENT LOOP
Validation has moved from static accuracy to **Pareto-optimal frontier mapping**.

*   **HAL Harness:** The standard measurement loop now involves replaying task statements against the **HAL Harness** to generate a "Cost-Success Pareto Frontier." 
*   **Causal Inference from Logs:** Research on **MoMA** (Mixture of Models and Agents, 2025) uses TOPSIS (Technique for Order of Preference by Similarity to Ideal Solution) to validate routers against historical execution logs, measuring how often the router picked the cheapest model that *would have* succeeded.
*   **Smallest Experiment:** A v0 is disproven if it cannot beat a "Fixed-Graph-Strong-Model" baseline on the **tau-bench** (policy adherence) with at least 30% cost savings at equivalent accuracy.

### 5. FAILURE MODES AND FALLBACKS
The primary failure mode in 2026 is **"Policy Drift."**

*   **ProbPol (2026):** This tool was developed to detect "Probabilistic Policy Conflicts" where multiple routing filters (e.g., safety vs. cost) fire simultaneously on a single input.
*   **SIRP (Semantic Inference Routing Protocol):** A proposed IETF standard (2026) aims to standardize fallbacks. If a router's confidence is $< 0.7$, the standard fallback is **"Escalation to Monolith"** (routing the entire task to the strongest frontier model, e.g., GPT-5 or Claude 4).
*   **Context Poisoning:** Research by Google DeepMind (2025) warns that "routed sub-tasks" can poison the shared context if the router picks a weak model that hallucinates early in a multi-step chain.

### 6. COST OF V0
For a solo founder, the v0 cost has dropped significantly due to **WRP (Workload-Router-Pool)** primitives.

*   **Time:** 1 Quarter (3 months) of solo effort. 
    *   *Month 1:* Data ingestion from **HAL** and **ToolACE**.
    *   *Month 2:* Fine-tuning a 1.5B "Small" Model (e.g., Llama-3-1B) on the **Domain-Action Taxonomy**.
    *   *Month 3:* Integration with a Semantic Proxy (like vLLM Semantic Router).
*   **Infrastructure:** Negligible. A fine-tuned 1.5B router can run on a single T4 or even consumer-grade hardware, with inference costs $1/100$th of the primary agent calls.
*   **The Marginal Effort Threshold:** The consensus (Chen et al., 2026) is that once a router achieves 90% of the Pareto frontier, marginal effort is better spent on **DSPy-style architecture optimization** rather than better routing classifiers.
