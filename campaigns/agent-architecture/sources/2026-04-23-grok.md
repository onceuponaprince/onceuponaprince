Yes, the position on the agent spectrum—context-rich monolithic LLM (one fat model juggling everything in a bloated window) versus granular single-task agents (narrow specialists with stripped, focused contexts)—can and should be chosen at the task level, not locked into a product-wide religion. Most teams won't admit this publicly because it kills the clean narrative sold to investors and makes routing a nightmare. The "correct" position shifts per task because monolithic shines on tightly coupled, sequential reasoning where information loss hurts, while granular wins on parallelizable, tool-heavy, or noisy work where excess context becomes rot or noise.⁠arXiv
