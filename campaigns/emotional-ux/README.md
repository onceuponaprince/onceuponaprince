# Emotional UX in LLMs

**Status: graduated to campaign 2026-04-30. Episode 1 (pilot) shipped. Episode 2 (ablation) in flight.**

## What this is

A living research campaign on whether the off-topic asides, hedges, mirroring, insight footers, and structural rhythm that appear in current-generation LLM outputs are load-bearing UX primitives, not stylistic decoration.

Started lateral on 2026-04-21 from Scene 05's post-conclusion note in the Command Centre campaign (`campaigns/command-centre/chapters/01-origin/scenes/05-two-side-projects-additional-workflow-infra.md`, "UX research note"). Episode 1 was framed as a pilot in the television sense — self-contained enough to stand alone, hinting at a wider premise. The pilot earned a second episode; the thread graduated to its own campaign at the point of Ep2 dispatch.

See `campaign.md` for the full campaign metadata, thesis, and episode index.

## How to read Episode 1

Open `pilot.html` in a browser. Single self-contained file: hybrid essay plus three interactive elements. No build step, no external dependencies.

- Warm vs cold comparison viewer (four example pairs)
- Mechanism spotter (hover the coloured phrases in the sample response)
- Warmth slider thought experiment (projects a hypothetical session-length curve — replaced with measured data once Episode 2 commits)
